ACNH GREENHOUSE

Visualize how flowers are born!

How Flower Reproduction Works

There are eight flower types in New Horizons: roses, tulips, pansies, cosmos, lillies, hyacinths, windflowers, and mums.

If two flowers of the same type are within a 3x3 area of each other, they can crossbreed to produce a new flower. Flowers in New Horizons can also self-duplicate, but that is not emulated on this website.

The following conditions must also be met for two flowers before they can breed:

  • They must be watered.
  • They must be at least in their second-last stage of development.
  • They cannot have produced an offspring that day already.
  • One must have empty space directly around them to produce a flower.

If all these conditions are met, the game rolls to decide if the two successfully breed. The chance of success is rolled for both flowers, and if one succeeds, they will breed. This chance starts at 5% and increases by 5% every day the flower has been watered, starting at four days in a row. People from other towns watering your plants also increases the chances of success by up to 75% (five unique visitors). All increases to the success rate are reset if the flower is dug up or successfully reproduces. The increases are also reset if the flower self-duplicates. (Self-duplication shares the same chance % as breeding, but it only occurs if there is no eligible mate in the area.)

The child's genes are decided using a Punnett Square, and each possible gene combination has an associated color. Unlike in previous games, there is no extra mutations that can happen in ACNH.

How to Use This Tool

Breeding flowers is fairly easy! (Here, anyway...)

You can edit the amount of rows/columns in the graph with the options at the top, since a 5x5 grid is fairly small. To reset the grid, just click "Update Grid." It will clear all the flowers currently there.

Breeding success rate changes exactly what it says it does. To make things simple, instead of the complicated calculation the game does with tracking each flower's consecutive days watered, each flower has the same chances of reproduction.

To place a flower on the grid, drag a flower from the "Seed Bags" or "Hybrid Island" sections onto the grid. Seed bag flowers are the ones you can buy from Nook's Cranny, and Hybrid Island flowers are flowers you can dig up and bring home from the mystery island full of hybrids.

Once a flower is on the grid, you can click on it to view more information about the flower. It will show you...

  • The flower's type and color.
  • The flower's origin (whether it is from seed bag, island, or breeding).
  • The flower's generation (what "day" it was born on). Flowers with generation 1 are from seed bags/islands.
  • The flower's genes. These decide color.
  • The flower's parents (if they are on the grid). You can click on a parent to put that parent in focus. Parents of focused flowers will be highlighted green on the grid.
  • The flower's children (if they are on the grid). You can click on a child to put it in foucs. Children of focused flowers will be highlighted blue on the grid.
  • The flowers possible children. This shows the possible colors that can result if a flower bred with each one of its neighbors.

You also have the ability to remove the flower.

If there is a flower that has spectacular genes (for example, you found a rose that could parent a blue rose) you can drag it to the "saved" section. Flowers in this section persist after the grid has been cleared, though they will be gone if you refresh the page.

To run the breeder, just click the "Pollinate" button and enjoy the results!
